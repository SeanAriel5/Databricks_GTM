Providence Health × Databricks
One of the largest non-profit health systems in the US leverages the Databricks Data Intelligence Platform to unify clinical, operational, and financial data — enabling population health analytics, HIPAA-compliant AI governance, and an agentic AI roadmap aimed at transforming clinical decision support and care coordination at scale.

Client Context

Providence Health & Services is one of the largest non-profit health systems in the United States, operating 51 hospitals, 1,000+ care sites, and serving more than 5 million patients annually across the Western US. As a mission-driven health system operating under strict HIPAA, HITECH, and state-level privacy regulations, Providence's data challenge combines the scale of a large enterprise with the governance requirements of the most regulated industry in the world: patient data cannot leave a compliant environment, models must be explainable and auditable, and access to sensitive clinical data must be controlled at the individual record level.

Providence partnered with Databricks to replace fragmented, hospital-level data silos with a unified Lakehouse — consolidating EHR data (Epic), clinical lab results, claims data, and operational metrics onto a single governed platform governed by Unity Catalog. The result: data science teams can build population health models and clinical AI applications at system-wide scale, not just per-hospital analyses, while Unity Catalog enforces HIPAA-required access controls and generates the audit logs required for compliance documentation.

Key Use Cases
Clinical Intelligence
Population Health Analytics
Delta Lake consolidates patient records, lab results, and claims data from 51 hospitals into a unified Lakehouse. Medallion Architecture Gold tables power risk stratification models that identify high-risk patient cohorts for proactive outreach — flagging patients likely to be readmitted within 30 days or deteriorating from chronic conditions. Models are trained on system-wide data, not hospital-specific silos, enabling statistically robust predictions across small patient subpopulations.
Compliance & Governance
HIPAA-Compliant AI Governance
Unity Catalog enforces row-level security on patient data — ensuring that analysts can only access records within their care site or authorized scope, and that de-identification is enforced at query time via dynamic data masking. Column-level encryption protects PII fields. Unity Catalog's automated audit logs satisfy HIPAA's access logging requirements without manual intervention — every query against patient data generates a tamper-resistant audit trail.
Operational Intelligence
Clinical Operations Optimization
Databricks SQL and Genie enable clinical operations teams to query bed utilization, OR scheduling efficiency, and supply chain consumption in natural language — without requiring SQL expertise. Lakeflow pipelines process real-time ADT (Admit/Discharge/Transfer) events from Epic to maintain near-real-time bed availability dashboards, enabling capacity management decisions based on live data rather than yesterday's reports.
Precision Medicine
Patient Outcomes Data Platform
Providence's data science team uses Databricks' ML suite (AutoML, MLflow, Feature Store) to build patient outcome prediction models. Feature Store enforces point-in-time correct feature retrieval — preventing data leakage where future information would contaminate training datasets, a critical compliance requirement for clinical ML. MLflow tracks all model versions, training datasets, and evaluation results for audit-readiness.

Agentic AI Roadmap
  • Clinical Decision Support Agent — Agent Bricks is being evaluated to build an autonomous clinical decision support system that monitors incoming lab results, cross-references against established clinical protocols stored in Vector Search, and proactively surfaces actionable recommendations to care teams — with full audit logging of every agent action through Unity AI Gateway to satisfy clinical documentation requirements.
  • Care Coordination Intelligence Agent — A multi-agent architecture is being designed to automate care coordination: an ingestion agent monitors discharge planning triggers from Epic, a recommendation agent identifies appropriate post-acute care settings based on patient risk profiles, and a communication agent drafts care transition documentation — compressing days of manual coordination to hours.
  • Revenue Cycle Optimization Agent — Providence is exploring an agentic system that monitors claim denial patterns in near-real-time, identifies root causes (missing documentation, coding errors, payer-specific rules), and generates corrective action recommendations — targeting a measurable reduction in denial rates and days-in-AR without increasing coding staff headcount.
