Client Context

7-Eleven is the world's largest convenience retail chain, operating over 83,000 stores across 19 countries. The scale and operational tempo of convenience retail — where store-level assortment decisions must be made daily based on hyper-local demand signals, weather, events, and competitor activity — creates one of the most complex data intelligence challenges in retail. With millions of transactions per day across a vast and highly localized footprint, 7-Eleven's data challenge is not just volume but velocity and locality: a product that sells out in a Tokyo suburb at 7 AM requires a different replenishment signal than one in a Dallas suburb at midnight.

7-Eleven partnered with Databricks to consolidate its fragmented store-level and regional data systems onto a unified Lakehouse, enabling data science teams to build store-level predictive models at a scale and refresh rate previously impossible with traditional data warehousing infrastructure.

Key Use Cases
Demand Intelligence
Hyper-Local Demand Forecasting
Delta Lake ingests hourly transaction data from 83,000+ stores and enriches it with external signals (weather, local events, competitor pricing, social media trends) via Lakeflow. Mosaic AI models generate store-SKU-level demand forecasts at daily granularity — identifying which products to over-stock for an upcoming heatwave in a specific zip code versus regular inventory levels.
Loyalty & Personalization
7REWARDS Program Intelligence
The 7REWARDS loyalty program generates billions of transaction events annually. Databricks consolidates app interactions, in-store purchases, and digital redemptions onto a unified customer profile in the Lakehouse. Vector Search powers real-time next-best-offer recommendations served to the mobile app, while MLflow tracks model performance across customer cohorts to prevent personalization drift.
Supply Chain
Autonomous Replenishment Signals
An agentic AI pipeline monitors real-time inventory levels (from POS integration via Lakeflow Connect), compares them against demand forecasts, and generates automated replenishment recommendations to distribution center operators — flagging exceptions that require human review. Agent Bricks orchestrates the multi-step process: forecast → gap analysis → supplier lookup → order generation.
Assortment Optimization
Dynamic Store-Level Assortment
Category managers use Databricks SQL and Genie to query store-level performance data in natural language: "Which stores in the Pacific Northwest are underperforming on cold beverage attach rate in the morning daypart?" ML models trained on Medallion Architecture Gold tables recommend assortment changes at the cluster-of-stores level, reducing the manual analysis time from weeks to hours.

Agentic AI Roadmap
  • Autonomous Promotional Planning Agent — Agent Bricks is being developed to automate the promotional calendar planning cycle: the agent ingests historical promotional lift data, competitor activity signals, and margin targets, and proposes an optimized promotional calendar for category managers to review and approve — compressing a 3-week planning cycle to under a day.
  • Franchise Operations Intelligence Agent — A multi-agent architecture is being designed to monitor franchisee performance anomalies (unexpected dips in transaction volume, waste spikes, staffing inefficiencies) in real time and proactively surface actionable recommendations to regional operations teams — reducing the time from signal to intervention from days to hours.
  • Real-Time Pricing Optimization — Following the Albertsons "merchant twin" model (Accenture + Databricks reference), 7-Eleven is evaluating an agentic pricing system that dynamically adjusts prepared food and beverage pricing based on daypart, weather, and local demand signals — driving margin improvement without manual intervention.

7-Eleven is the prototypical "Agentic Reinvention" proof point for convenience retail and high-frequency brick-and-mortar businesses. The combination of extreme data volume (millions of daily transactions), hyper-local variability (83,000 unique micro-markets), and the need for autonomous decision-making at scale (impossible to staff category managers for every store cluster) makes Databricks' agentic AI stack — Agent Bricks, Mosaic AI, Genie, and Unity Catalog — the only viable path to intelligent operations at this scale. For Accenture, 7-Eleven opens doors to any large-format retailer, convenience chain, or QSR operator asking: "How do we manage store-level AI decisions at enterprise scale?"
