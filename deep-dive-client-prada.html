Client Context

Prada Group is the world's most prestigious luxury fashion conglomerate, operating Prada, Miu Miu, Church's, and Car Shoe across 650+ directly operated stores in over 70 countries. Luxury fashion presents a data challenge unlike any other retail sector: the product catalog is intentionally small and exclusive (limited-edition drops vs. mass retail's tens of thousands of SKUs), the customer base is globally dispersed but individually known (ultra-high-net-worth clients expect deeply personalized relationships, not generic recommendations), and sustainability reporting is now a legal requirement under EU CSRD regulations — demanding supply chain provenance data at the material and supplier level.

Prada Group partnered with Databricks to consolidate fragmented client data across in-store clienteling systems (often paper-based in luxury), e-commerce, and CRM into a unified intelligence platform — enabling the same level of personalization that the world's best boutique sales associates provide intuitively, but powered by data science and delivered at global scale. The Databricks Lakehouse also handles Prada's most pressing 2026 mandate: CSRD-compliant scope 1/2/3 emissions reporting, requiring supply chain provenance data governed by Unity Catalog.

Key Use Cases
Client Intelligence
Ultra-Luxury Personalization Platform
Delta Lake consolidates in-store clienteling records, e-commerce behavior, CRM events, and event attendance (fashion shows, private sales) into unified ultra-high-net-worth client profiles. Mosaic AI models compute purchase propensity for upcoming collections, preferred communication channels, and size/fit preferences — enabling personal shoppers to prepare tailored product selections before client appointments. Vector Search powers semantic search across the product catalog for stylist assistance.
Supply Chain Intelligence
Global Limited-Edition Planning
Lakeflow integrates global manufacturing, logistics, and inventory data — enabling demand-aware production planning for limited-run collections where over-production destroys brand exclusivity and under-production means lost revenue and disappointed clients. ML models trained on historical collection performance and waitlist data predict optimal production quantities by market and channel, replacing the purely intuition-driven allocation decisions made by regional merchandise planners.
ESG & Compliance
CSRD Sustainability Reporting
Unity Catalog governs scope 1, 2, and 3 emissions data and supply chain provenance records — enabling automated CSRD-compliant reporting across Prada Group's multi-brand, multi-country manufacturing footprint. Delta Lake's time travel capability provides the historical emissions baseline required for year-over-year comparison reporting. Unity Catalog lineage traces each sustainability metric back to its source supplier data, satisfying the EU CSRD's auditability requirements without manual data reconciliation.
Trend Intelligence
Cultural Trend Signal Detection
Lakeflow pipelines ingest global social media signals, runway coverage, and editorial mentions into a Medallion Architecture bronze layer. Foundation Model APIs via Mosaic AI perform semantic analysis — identifying emerging cultural themes, color stories, and material preferences before they peak in mainstream media. Genie provides design team researchers with natural language access to trend data: "What is the sentiment trajectory for metallics in APAC across the last 18 months?"

Agentic AI Roadmap
  • Autonomous Replenishment Agent for Flagship Boutiques — Agent Bricks is being evaluated to automate stock replenishment decisions for flagship stores: the agent monitors real-time inventory via Lakeflow Connect, compares against demand forecasts and upcoming event calendars (fashion weeks, VIP events), and generates prioritized replenishment recommendations with supplier routing — reducing the manual effort of boutique stock coordinators while improving product availability for top clients.
  • Personalized Digital Styling Agent — A client-facing Agent Bricks application being designed to provide Prada's digital clients with the experience of a personal shopping assistant: the agent accesses the client's purchase history and preferences (governed by Unity Catalog), combines them with the current collection and Vector Search over product descriptions, and generates curated styling recommendations delivered via the Prada app — extending the in-boutique personal shopping experience to the digital channel.
  • CSRD Automated Reporting Agent — An agentic pipeline is being developed to automate the quarterly CSRD report generation cycle: the agent ingests updated supplier emissions data via Lakeflow, validates against Unity Catalog lineage for completeness, runs scope 3 calculation models, and generates draft report sections — compressing a 6-week manual reporting cycle to days and improving data accuracy through automated validation.
