Client Context

Adidas is one of the world's largest sportswear companies, operating across 160+ countries with thousands of direct retail touchpoints, digital commerce channels, and third-party wholesale partners. The complexity of managing product lifecycles, inventory positions, and consumer demand signals across this footprint creates a massive data integration and intelligence challenge. Adidas partnered with Databricks to consolidate its fragmented data landscape onto a unified Lakehouse, enabling data science teams to operate at the pace and scale that global retail demands.

Key Use Cases
Supply Chain
Demand Forecasting at Scale
Adidas uses Delta Lake to ingest and unify point-of-sale, e-commerce, and wholesale order data into a single Bronze-to-Gold Medallion pipeline. ML models trained on this unified estate generate SKU-level demand forecasts across seasons, geographies, and channels — replacing fragmented regional forecasting systems.
Digital Commerce
Personalization Engine
Mosaic AI's Vector Search and Foundation Model APIs power the real-time product recommendation engine on adidas.com and the Adidas app. User embeddings are computed on Databricks and served at low latency, enabling hyper-personalized product discovery across 30+ million active digital members.
Governance
Multi-Cloud Data Governance
Unity Catalog governs data access across Adidas' AWS and Azure deployments, ensuring GDPR compliance for European consumer data, enforcing row-level security by regional data ownership, and providing automated lineage from source systems to business dashboards.
Sustainability
ESG & Carbon Reporting
Adidas consolidates sustainability metrics (carbon footprint by product, supply chain emissions, material sourcing) on the Lakehouse. Unity Catalog's lineage tracking ensures audit-ready reporting for ESG disclosures, eliminating manual spreadsheet consolidation across 200+ supplier data feeds.

Agentic AI Roadmap
  • Autonomous Merchandising Agent (Evaluating) — Agent Bricks is being piloted to build an autonomous merchandising agent that ingests real-time sell-through data, identifies underperforming SKUs, and proactively recommends markdown, reallocation, or promotional actions to merchandising teams — reducing manual intervention and improving margin.
  • Real-Time Stock Optimization — A multi-agent pipeline is being designed to monitor inventory imbalances across distribution centers and trigger automated reorder signals using Databricks Workflows + Agent Bricks, reducing out-of-stock events during peak trading periods.
  • Supplier Intelligence Agent — Planned agent to monitor supplier delivery performance, quality metrics, and sustainability scores in real time, surfacing compliance risks before they impact production timelines.
